Alejandro Miyashiro was born and raised in a multicultural home in San Diego, California where he grew up developing photography skills and later earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Film Production at San Diego State University. Growing up on the border of two countries, stories of diversity and the power of filmmaking combined with his love for the camera led him to become a cinematographer. Alejandro is a Wasserman Scholar and a recipient of The Fusion and The National Hispanic Foundation for The Arts emerging filmmaker award. He has shot narrative films that have been screened in the U.S. and internationally; including a short film that recently had its world premiere at the Festival de Cannes. His other work consists of music videos and commercial work shot on various formats ranging from 16mm film to the latest digital cinema cameras. Alejandro is currently based in Brooklyn, NY completing a Master’s Degree at New York University’s Graduate Film Program.

An NYU Tisch alum, Emily Wong received a BFA in Film & Television Production. In her time in college, she created a number of short documentaries, while concentrating in documentary and cinematography. Her first short "Getting Banksy" grabbed the attention of HBO Documentary Films. They licensed some of her footage, which was included in HBO's "Banksy Does New York." Her second short "Into the Caves" was shot in Cuba right before the turning point in US-Cuba relations, right before they restored diplomatic relationships. It was featured on Cornell University's website. Her senior thesis "Sofar Sounds: A Documentary" is currently in post-production. When she is not filming and editing her own projects, Emily freelances as a camera operator and AC, and works as a Video Lead at Sofar Sounds. She has shot for short films, documentaries, music videos, and commercials, working for Dow Jones, Sally Hansen, the Whitney Museum, Cosmo, and COTY, Inc. She has AC-ed on commercials for Squarespace, eBay, Estée Lauder, and LG Electronics.

Margaret Wiss is interested in the interaction between dance and science. As a choreographer and artist, she wishes to reveal the invisible motion of physical forces in the world. Attracted to dynamic movement, which investigates the perception of dance as a scientific sport, she explores the interactions of dance and the environment – inside and outside the body. She has performed at multiple venues, notably The Kennedy Center and Boston’s Institute of Contemporary Art, and has worked with Pilobolus Dance Theater as well as choreographers Jennifer Hart, Kinsun Chan, and Adele Myers. Her work has been presented across the United States. In 2017, she collaborated with visual artist, Noël Hennelly for Norte Maar's Production, CounterPointe5 in Brooklyn, NY. She has choreographed for PDX Contemporary Ballet, North Atlantic Dance Theatre, The Harvard Ballet Company, and the Dance BARN Festival. She has taught for Mount Holyoke College and choreographed for the Five College Dance Department. She is a member of the International Association for Dance Medicine and Science. A graduate of Mount Holyoke College, magna cum laude, she received a Bachelor of Arts in Dance Kinesiology with high honors from the dance department. She is currently pursuing a Master in Fine Arts in Dance at Tisch School for the Arts.

He has produced and edited over 25 original movie musicals for the New York Film Academy.

Will Mayo is a director, cinematographer, and sound designer. He grew up on a horse farm in Thompson’s Station, Tennessee and went on to attend New York University, where he received a BFA in Film and Television Production in 2013. As a cinematographer he’s shot a number of award-winning films, most notably the acclaimed Irish-language short, AN CRANN, directed by Emma Carlson. The film won the prestigious 1st Prize King Award at NYU’s First Run Film Festival and the National Board of Review Student Filmmaker Grant. His most recent directorial effort, FIRST NIGHT OUT, is a premiering at NewFest: New York's LGBT Film Festival, and is premiering internationally at Mix Brasil, the oldest and most prestigious LGBT film festival in Latin America. His experimental student work garnered him acclaim from NYU and beyond, most notably from io9, which described his thesis, THIS IS HOW YOU HAUNT YOUR HOUSE, as a fabulously grim short horror story. As a sound designer, he's designed two features and a half-dozen shorts. He resides in Brooklyn.
