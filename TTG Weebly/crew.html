www.willmayofilm.com

Will Mayo is a director, cinematographer, and sound designer. He grew up on a horse farm in Thompson’s Station, Tennessee and went on to attend New York University, where he received a BFA in Film and Television Production in 2013. As a cinematographer he’s shot a number of award-winning films, most notably the acclaimed Irish-language short, AN CRANN, directed by Emma Carlson. The film won the prestigious 1st Prize King Award at NYU’s First Run Film Festival and the National Board of Review Student Filmmaker Grant. His most recent directorial effort, FIRST NIGHT OUT, is a premiering at NewFest: New York's LGBT Film Festival, and is premiering internationally at Mix Brasil, the oldest and most prestigious LGBT film festival in Latin America. His experimental student work garnered him acclaim from NYU and beyond, most notably from io9, which described his thesis, THIS IS HOW YOU HAUNT YOUR HOUSE, as a fabulously grim short horror story. As a sound designer, he's designed two features and a half-dozen shorts. He resides in Brooklyn.

Contact Will at willmayofilm@gmail.com

www.isabella-tan.com

Isabella was born in Taiwan and raised in Malaysia until she began attending NYU Tisch School of the Arts for film and TV production. Isabella is now an aspiring Director and Cinematographer based out of New York City with extensive travel experience and an international background. Aside from her film work, Isabella freelances as a notable fashion and portrait photographer. She largely directs and shoots music videos, short films and documentaries. Her short film, “Girl Who Reads” was an official selection for National Film Festival for Talented Youths (NFFTY) in 2015. As a largely visual director and cinematographer, she creates stories with a sense of surrealism and tries to focus on conveying complicated human emotions.
